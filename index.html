Cellular Automata

Welcome!

This generator demonstrates three different types of cellular automata: square, triangular, and hexagonal. In each generator, an individual cell starts as either dead or alive. Whether the cell lives or dies on the next iteration of the game depends upon how many of its immediate neighbors are dead or alive. The rules vary for each shape.
The square cellular automaton is simply a recreation of Conway's Game of Life.
The triangular cellular automaton is known as Rule 90. Beginning with one live will result in a Sierpinski's Triangle as the number of iterations approaches infinity.
The hexagonal cellular automaton is a direct variation on Conway's Game of Life, simply with a slight variation in the defintion of "neighbor" on this hexagonally tiled grid.
